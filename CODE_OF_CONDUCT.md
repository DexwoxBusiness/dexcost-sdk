# DexCost Community Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want DexCost to be a practical, respectful place for developers building AI
cost attribution systems across Python, TypeScript, Rust, and Go. We pledge to
make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing criticism on the technical work, not the person.
- Being clear about tradeoffs, uncertainty, and compatibility impact.
- Helping contributors understand cross-SDK parity requirements before a change
  becomes frustrating.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention, or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Dismissing vulnerability reports, bug reports, or contributor questions in a
  hostile or humiliating way.
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional
  open-source community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub issues,
pull requests, discussions, code review, release coordination, community chat,
and public communication when an individual is representing the DexCost project.

## Enforcement

Report Code of Conduct concerns to conduct@dexcost.io. Please include:

- A short description of what happened.
- Links, screenshots, or message IDs when available.
- The names or handles of people involved.
- Whether there is any immediate safety or privacy concern.

The DexCost maintainers will review reports promptly and privately. We will only
share report details with people who need the information to investigate and
respond.

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including a private
warning, temporary limitation of participation, removal of comments, closing or
locking threads, revoking repository access, or a permanent ban from project
spaces.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and
for taking fair, consistent corrective action when needed. Maintainers should
avoid conflicts of interest when handling reports. If a report involves a
maintainer, another maintainer or company representative should handle the
review.
